Introduction to Physics

Learn the basics of mechanics, energy, and motion to excel in your freshman year.

About This Course

This course introduces freshman students to the fundamentals of physics, covering mechanics, energy, and motion. Designed to build a strong foundation, it prepares you for advanced science courses and real-world applications.

What You'll Learn

  • Understand Newton's laws of motion and their applications.
  • Analyze forces and energy in physical systems.
  • Solve problems involving kinematics and dynamics.
  • Apply physics concepts to everyday scenarios.

Physics Course Units

Fundamentals of Physics

This comprehensive course covers all major physics concepts from mechanics to electromagnetism. The textbook provides detailed explanations, examples, and practice problems for each unit.

Unit 1: Preliminaries

  • 1.1 Physical Quantities and Measurement

    Understanding fundamental physical quantities and their measurement techniques.

  • 1.2 Uncertainty in Measurement

    Significant digits and error analysis in physical measurements.

  • 1.3 Vectors

    Composition, resolution, and operations with vectors.

Unit 2: Kinematics and Dynamics

  • 2.1 Kinematics in One and Two Dimensions

    Motion analysis including displacement, velocity, and acceleration.

  • 2.2 Particle Dynamics

    Newton's laws of motion and applications.

  • 2.3 Work, Energy and Momentum

    Conservation principles in mechanical systems.

Unit 3: Fluid Mechanics

  • 3.1 Properties of Bulk Matter

    Characteristics of materials in bulk states.

  • 3.2 Density and Pressure

    Fluid statics and pressure calculations.

  • 3.3 Fluid Dynamics

    Bernoulli's equation and moving fluids.

Unit 4: Heat and Thermodynamics

  • 4.1 Temperature Concepts

    Thermodynamic equilibrium and temperature scales.

  • 4.2 Thermal Expansion

    Effects of temperature on material dimensions.

  • 4.3 Heat Transfer

    Mechanisms of thermal energy transfer.

Unit 5: Oscillations and Waves

  • 5.1 Simple Harmonic Motion

    Periodic motion analysis and applications.

  • 5.2 Wave Characteristics

    Properties and behavior of mechanical waves.

  • 5.3 Optics

    Image formation with lenses and mirrors.

Unit 6: Electromagnetism

  • 6.1 Electric Fields

    Coulomb's law and electric potential.

  • 6.2 Circuits

    Current, resistance, and Ohm's law.

  • 6.3 Electronics

    Semiconductors, diodes, and transistors.

Unit 7: Physics Applications

  • 7.1 Physics in Technology

    Applications in industries and power generation.

  • 7.2 Medical Physics

    Imaging technologies and health applications.

  • 7.3 Space Science

    Astrophysics and earth science applications.
